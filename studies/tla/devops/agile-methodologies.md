## Agile methodologies

### What are agile methodologies in software development?

**Agile** is a way of working where teams:

- **Deliver software in small pieces, frequently** (instead of one big delivery at the end).  
- **Adapt to change quickly** (requirements can change, and that’s OK).  
- **Work closely with the customer** (get feedback often and adjust).  
- **Collaborate as a team** (devs, testers, product, etc. work together).

In short: **Agile = build a bit, show it, learn, adjust, repeat**, instead of planning everything perfectly up front.

---

### Common agile frameworks

#### Scrum

- Work in short cycles called **sprints** (usually 1–2 weeks).  
- Has clear **roles**:  
  - **Product Owner** – decides priorities and what brings value.  
  - **Scrum Master** – helps the team follow Scrum and remove blockers.  
  - **Development Team** – builds, tests, delivers the product.  
- Uses **ceremonies**:  
  - **Daily stand‑up** – quick daily sync (o que fiz ontem, hoje, bloqueios).  
  - **Sprint planning** – decide what will be done in the sprint.  
  - **Sprint review** – show what was built, get feedback.  
  - **Retrospective** – discuss what went well, what to improve.

#### Kanban

- Visual board with columns like **To Do → Doing → Done**.  
- Focus on **limiting work in progress (WIP)** and improving **flow**.  
- No fixed sprints: work items move through the board continuously.  
- Good for **support/maintenance** teams and continuous delivery.
